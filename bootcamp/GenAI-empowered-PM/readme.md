## 🔁 Example Workflows You Could Build

You do not need to invent something new. The goal is to take a real, recurring task from your working life and make it run faster — or automatically — with AI. Here are examples of workflows attendees have built in similar sessions:

### Example 1 — Automated meeting follow-up
```
Meeting recording / transcript
    → Claude or ChatGPT (summarise + extract action items)
    → Notion or Confluence (create meeting note page)
    → Jira or Asana (create tickets from action items)
    → Slack (notify the team with a summary)
```

### Example 2 — Requirements drafting assistant
```
Stakeholder interview notes (uploaded by PM/BA)
    → Claude (generate structured user stories & acceptance criteria)
    → Google Docs or Confluence (populate a requirements template)
    → Email notification sent to the project team
```

### Example 3 — Weekly status report generator
```
Project tracker data (Jira / Asana / Monday.com)
    → Make or Zapier (fetch this week's completed and in-progress items)
    → ChatGPT or Claude (draft a formatted status report)
    → Email or Slack (send report to stakeholders automatically)
```

### Example 4 — Risk & issue log assistant
```
Daily standup notes or meeting transcript
    → Claude (identify risks, blockers, and decisions)
    → Spreadsheet or Notion database (log with priority and owner)
    → Slack alert (flag high-priority risks to the project lead)
```

### 1. Claude by Anthropic

Claude is a powerful AI assistant from Anthropic, excellent for writing, summarising, and analysing complex documents and requirements.

#### Create a free Claude account

1. Go to [https://claude.ai](https://claude.ai)
2. Click **Sign up** and create a free account using your email or Google account
3. Verify your email address

#### Explore the free tier

The free Claude plan gives you access to Claude Sonnet, which is fully capable for workshop activities — document drafting, summarisation, requirements writing, and analysis. No credit card is required for the free tier.

#### Install the Claude desktop app (recommended)

Having the Claude desktop app makes it easier to work alongside other applications:

1. Go to [https://claude.ai/download](https://claude.ai/download)
2. Download the version for your operating system (Windows or macOS)
3. Install and sign in with your Claude account

#### Try a quick test

Log in and try this prompt to get a feel for how Claude works:

```
I am a Project Manager running a software delivery project.
Draft a concise weekly status report for stakeholders based on the following:
- Completed this week: User authentication feature, code review sessions
- In progress: Payment integration (60% done), testing environment setup
- Blocked: Waiting for API credentials from the vendor
- Risks: Vendor delay may impact the release date by 3–5 days
```

### 2. ChatGPT by OpenAI

ChatGPT is one of the most widely used AI assistants, great for brainstorming, Q&A, drafting, and general analysis.

#### Create a free ChatGPT account

1. Go to [https://chat.openai.com](https://chat.openai.com)
2. Click **Sign up** and create a free account
3. Verify your email address

#### Free vs Plus

The free tier (GPT-3.5) is sufficient for the workshop. If you already have a Plus subscription (GPT-4o), even better — but it is not required.

#### Try a quick test

Log in and try this prompt:

```
I am a Business Analyst. Generate 5 user stories in standard format
(As a [user], I want [goal], so that [benefit]) for a mobile app
that helps field technicians log equipment maintenance visits.
Include acceptance criteria for each story.
```

### 4. Make (No-Code Automation Platform)

Make (formerly Integromat) is the primary no-code automation platform for the workshop. You will use it to visually connect AI tools and build your workflow.

#### Create a free Make account

1. Go to [https://www.make.com](https://www.make.com)
2. Click **Get started free**
3. Sign up with your email address
4. Verify your email — no credit card required for the free tier

#### Explore the interface (5 minutes)

After signing in:
- Click **Create a new scenario**
- Browse the app integrations panel (search for tools you already use — Slack, Gmail, Notion, Jira, etc.)
- Familiarise yourself with the concept of **modules** (each step in a workflow) and **connections** (the links between apps)

The free plan includes 1,000 operations per month, which is more than enough for the workshop and initial daily use.

---

### 5. Zapier (Alternative No-Code Automation)

Zapier is another leading no-code automation platform, known for its simplicity and wide app library.

#### Create a free Zapier account

1. Go to [https://zapier.com](https://zapier.com)
2. Click **Sign up for free**
3. Register with your email or Google account
4. No credit card required for the free tier (100 tasks/month)

#### Familiarise yourself with Zaps

After signing in:
- Click **Create** → **Zaps**
- Explore the **trigger** and **action** concept — a trigger is an event that starts the workflow, and an action is what happens as a result
- Browse the app directory to see which tools you use are already connected
